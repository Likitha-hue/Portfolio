UI / UX Case Study

Healthy Food Hub App

"Your root to healthy living."

Platform  →  Mobile App (Android & iOS)
Role  →  UI/UX Designer
Duration  →  12 Weeks

Problem Statement

In today's fast-paced urban lifestyle, health-conscious individuals struggle to consistently access nutritious, freshly prepared meals. Existing food delivery platforms prioritize convenience over nutrition — offering little to no calorie transparency, no personalized health tracking, and zero motivation for sustained healthy habits. Users are left overwhelmed, uninformed, and unsupported in their wellness journey.

Possible Solution

LifeRoot is a comprehensive healthy food hub mobile app that bridges the gap between nutritious eating and everyday convenience. It combines a curated healthy menu, AI-powered ingredient scanning, a recipe library, a health snack store, and a gamified rewards system — all in one platform — empowering users to eat well, track nutrition, and stay motivated daily.

Target Audience

  • Health-conscious young professionals aged 22–35
  • Fitness enthusiasts & gym-goers tracking macros
  • College students managing diet on a budget
  • Home cooks seeking nutritious Indian recipe inspiration

The Approach

Following the Design Thinking framework, I began with deep user empathy — conducting interviews and surveys to uncover real pain points. I then defined personas, mapped user journeys, and structured the information architecture. Paper wireframes and low-fidelity prototypes were iterated before developing the high-fidelity visual design, followed by usability testing and final refinements.

Process

Design Thinking Process

LifeRoot was designed using a full 5-phase Design Thinking approach — ensuring every decision was grounded in real user needs, validated insights, and iterative refinement before the final visual design was produced.

Empathize Phase

Understanding the User

Qualitative Research

Conducted in-depth interviews with 8 health-conscious users aged 20–35 to understand their daily food habits, pain points, and unmet needs around healthy eating and nutrition tracking.

Interview Questions

1. How do you currently manage your daily diet and nutrition intake?
2. What challenges do you face when trying to eat healthy consistently?
3. Have you used a food app for healthy meals? Describe your experience.
4. How important is knowing the calorie/nutritional value of your food?
5. Would you use an AI tool that scans ingredients and suggests healthy recipes?

Key Insights Derived

Most users rely on guesswork for nutrition — no structured tracking system.
Existing food apps don't filter by health goals or show macros upfront.
Users want gamification to stay consistent — streaks and rewards would help.
AI-powered tools for ingredient scanning were rated "very useful" by all participants.

Quantitative Research

Conducted an online survey with 50 respondents aged 18–40 to validate qualitative findings and identify patterns across a broader user base.

User Persona

Based on research insights, I created two primary user personas representing the core target audience of LifeRoot — a fitness-driven young professional and a health-conscious college student — to guide all design decisions throughout the project.

Devanth works long hours at a tech firm and often skips meals or grabs unhealthy snacks out of convenience. He recently joined a gym and wants to align his diet with his fitness goals — but existing food apps offer him zero guidance. He's comfortable with technology and wants a smart, all-in-one solution for healthy eating and nutrition tracking.

She'd love a platform that combines exploration and practicality in one place.
